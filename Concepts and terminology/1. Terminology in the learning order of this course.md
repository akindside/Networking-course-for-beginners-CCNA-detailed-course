#Terminology
###### Imperative :
an essential or urgent thing.
###### disparate :
essentially different in kind; not able to be compared.
###### underwent (present : to undergo) :

###### siloed :
(of a system, process, department, etc.) isolated from others.
###### framework :
a basic structure underlying a system, concept, or text.
###### tandem :
a bicycle with seats and [pedals](https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&hs=sF59&sca_esv=ee81a51e8e25352b&channel=entpr&biw=1342&bih=633&sxsrf=ANbL-n5xwyRnjpHL7AHt3tjNU06JLarAtg:1772027792899&q=pedals&si=AL3DRZGftPMu5S1DRQlTjs_j9BL7c0tc4mthrzIAfdjcfAkXE6L6CXPwQ3kGb8IWzEJ_q6Wh9Pf6LsxpTwBbu-VFDQx2ZOCD-w%3D%3D&expnd=1&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiq1IL55fSSAxXZnf0HHcsnO-kQyecJegQIHxAQ) for two riders, one behind the other.

###### ubiquitous :
present, appearing, or found everywhere.
###### opaque :
not able to be seen through; not transparent.
###### tangible :
[perceptible](https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&hs=xG59&sca_esv=ee81a51e8e25352b&channel=entpr&biw=1342&bih=633&sxsrf=ANbL-n6kXw2HJqXyJ64M9VK4pK-cjMBWEA:1772031811982&q=perceptible&si=AL3DRZE_xiDg-d6tsHNNJuabyRZWY8SrIJ3nxMGXz0FVwF9xhwj_Ll2FPoccnmYUcxIH1gZL_L0piZq-sEzw6HTzoCvsF5BRPgFtpVvmSAUqFLRhxlEBdDk%3D&expnd=1&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwj4-rv19PSSAxXkklYBHW4dN4AQyecJegQIHxAQ) by touch.
###### lingua franca :
a language that is adopted as a common language between speakers whose native languages are different.
###### novice :
a person new to and [inexperienced](https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&hs=FH59&sca_esv=ee81a51e8e25352b&channel=entpr&biw=1342&bih=633&sxsrf=ANbL-n58W6CLof0cB4PG_PS7ueBO1uXNjQ:1772032889094&q=inexperienced&si=AL3DRZGg9khW3vp7wLr2OX__wrfhedbrBVcOmfNH7VVISZXkMYZOjGrdYLZD8chJOZ77AYY0-M6KK267zE-d4j_u1w1sd1O3aY19JqJSWe8HBmUMnJVdI3Y%3D&expnd=1&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwi224n3-PSSAxUy9LsIHRkUAtkQyecJegQIHxAQ) in a job or situation.
###### rigorous :
extremely thorough and careful.
###### paradigm :
A model, pattern, or standard, often serving as a shared set of assumptions in science or culture
###### latency :
Latency in networking is the time delay experienced when data travels from its source to its destination
###### overhaul :
a thorough examination of machinery or a system, with repairs or changes made if necessary

###### electromagnetic interference (EMI) :
the disruption of electronic device operation caused by unwanted electromagnetic energy
###### data framing :
a Data Link Layer (Layer 2) process in networking that organizes raw, continuous bitstreams from the Physical Layer into distinct, manageable units called **frames**. It allows the ***receiver*** to identify where a frame begins and ends, which is crucial for distinguishing frames in a continuous bitstream.
###### hardware circuitry :
the physical, interconnected network of electronic components—such as transistors, resistors, and capacitors—through which electricity flows to perform specific functions in a device.
###### deploy :
To install, test and implement a computer system or application.
