The **International Organization for Standardization (ISO)** is an independent, non-governmental international organization that develops and publishes **standards** to ensure the quality, safety, and efficiency of products, services, and systems.

Think of ISO as the world’s "Rulebook Maker." Because of ISO, a credit card works in any ATM, and a nut from one country fits a bolt from another.

---

### Why ISO Matters (For Beginners)

Before ISO, every country and company had its own way of doing things. This made international trade nearly impossible. ISO solves this by creating a "common language" for industries.

- **Consistency:** Whether you buy a lightbulb in Japan or Germany, the base is the same size because of ISO standards.
    
- **Safety:** ISO standards for medical devices and food safety (like ISO 22000) ensure that products won't harm you.
    
- **Trust:** When you see "ISO 9001 Certified" on a company's wall, it's a "seal of approval" telling you they follow strict quality management rules.
    

---

### Famous Standards You Should Know

ISO has published over **25,000 standards** covering almost everything. Here are the most common ones:

|**Standard**|**What it covers**|**Why it’s used**|
|---|---|---|
|**ISO 9001**|Quality Management|To prove a company consistently makes good products.|
|**ISO/IEC 27001**|Information Security|To protect data and prevent hacking.|
|**ISO 14001**|Environmental Management|To help companies reduce their carbon footprint.|
|**ISO 45001**|Occupational Health & Safety|To keep workers safe on the job.|
|**ISO 3166**|Country Codes|Why we use "US" for USA or "FR" for France.|

### How it Relates to Your Previous Questions

You asked about the **OSI Model** earlier. It is important to know that **ISO** (the organization) created the **OSI** (the Open Systems Interconnection) model.

- **ISO:** The group of people who decide the rules.
    
- **OSI:** The specific 7-layer rulebook they wrote for networking.
    

### Quick Facts

- **Name Origin:** "ISO" is not an acronym for the English name. It comes from the Greek word _isos_, meaning **"equal."** This way, the name is the same in every language.
    
- **Membership:** It is a network of national standards bodies from over **160 countries**.
    
- **Headquarters:** Located in **Geneva, Switzerland**.
